Author Guidelines:


Manuscript should be prepared according to the JIDSE guidelines and can be submitted under any of the following heads:
• Original articles: Original research including observational, experimental, epidemiological, analytical research, Clinical trials and research related to Dental education. Word limit:
• Review articles: Literature review, systematic review, scoping review, meta-analysis etc. Word limit:
• Case reports: Rare cases, Interdisciplinary cases, Cases with some novel treatment modalities etc. Word limit:
• Dental Innovations: Any new technique in dental treatment or dental innovation in dental education. Word limit:
• Clinical images: Well documented special cases with Images of high Quality and good resolution. Word limit:
• Newsletters:
Submitting manuscript: Manuscript should be submitted to jidsejournal@gmail.com

Preparation of manuscript:


1. Document should be prepared in English as Microsoft word document with Font Arial/Times New Roman, size 12, double spacing under following headings.
2. Title page: including Title of manuscript, Author’s Names and their affiliations, Conflict of interest statement.
3. Abstract: Should be short (250 words) and must include aim, material and methods, results and conclusion.
4. Key words: 5-7 relevant to subject, standard Mesh terms
5. Introduction: It should be brief, provide background and justify/ rationalize the need of research.
6. Materials and Methods: Should be written in detail including selection and characteristics of subjects, sample size, apparatus used, techniques, procedures etc. For clinical trials the procedures followed were in accordance with the Helsinki Declaration of 1975 that was revised in 2013 and institutional ethical committee, should be included. Authors submitting review articles should include the methods used for locating, selecting, extracting, and synthesizing data. Used statistical methods should be clearly mentioned.
7. Results : Should be illustrated using Tables, graphs, charts etc. Results should be presented in sequential manner and repetition should be avoided.
8. Discussion: Avoid repeating results in discussion. Emphasize your key outcomes. Discuss your results in the light of present knowledge and existing literature. Limitations of current research and future recommendations can be included.
9. Acknowledments: Acknowledments regarding statistical help, material help, technical help etc. can be included.
10. References: Bibliography should be in Vancouver style and journal name should be abbreviated. Wherever necessary relevant references should be included but no references are needed in abstract. E.g.
11. Tables and figures: All tables and Figures should be captioned and numbered according to the citation in the text like Table1 or Fig 1etc. Tables and Figures should not be embedded in the body of the text. These can be placed either at the end of the document or in separate document. Written permission from original source is needed to duplicate tables and figures and a credit line should be included. For footnotes, use of symbols: *, †, ‡, §, ||,**, ††, ‡‡ should be done in sequence. The Journal reserves the right to crop, rotate, reduce, or enlarge the photographs to the desired size.


Important Instructions:


1. Declaration of conflict of interest: A statement regarding conflict of interest should be submitted along with all manuscripts at the time of submission.
2. Plagiarism: Submitted manuscript should be free from plagiarism and authors of manuscript will be completely responsible for that.
3. Manuscript should not be submitted to any other journal simultaneously. Author must disclose if some part of work has already been published or under consideration elsewhere.
4. Certificate from Institutions ethical committee should also be submitted along with original research and clinical trials.
5. If research involves animal subjects, then also ethical clearance certificate has to be submitted.
6. To justify authorship for the submitted manuscripts, the contributors should either have contributed in conception and design, acquisition of data, or analysis and interpretation of data or drafting the article or revising it.
7. Once submitted, change of order of authorship, addition or deletion of any author is possible only after written consent of all the contributors.
8. Identifying information of a patient or subject can be published only after obtaining a written consent from the patient or subject for publication and this should be mentioned in the manuscript and should be submitted to journal.
9. Copyright: Once published manuscript with all its content is copyright of its publishers. Copyright transfer form will be signed and submitted by all authors to journal before publication.
